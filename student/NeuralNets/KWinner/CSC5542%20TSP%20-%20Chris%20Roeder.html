  4. Convergence
    The code stops when either the number of iterations hits 10,000 or  when all the neurons' activation levels reach a threshold. Values of 0.7 usually stopped the network before 10,000 iterations, but sometimes a little too early. Energy levels vary too widely to be used, though the graph usually levels out long before  10,000 iterations.  An effective stopping strategy might be one that  looks at changes in the system energy and stops running when the changes become very small.

Application User's Guide

The application provided shows the progress of the Fuzzy TSP Neural Net solution with three graphic displays.  Various controls at the bottom of the application allow the user to choose the initial configuration, the simulation speed, and the display scale.

The graphic displays show in order from left to right, the activation of  each neuron, the fuzzy  read out for the developing tour and the energy level of the whole network. A text display in the bottom center shows the current energy level, iteration number and tour length.

Controls just below the displays on the left allow the user to slow the simulation by increasing the size of a pause in each iteration. The size of the pause in milliseconds is displayed to the left of a slider that can be adjusted from 1 to 1000 milliseconds.

Below the delay time control are a pair of radio buttons that  control the displays. The activations as well as the energy can be displayed in either logarithmic or linear formats. The scales of the graphs adjust with the choice. If the user chooses a linear display, the activation display
can be set to one of three sensitivities or scales labeled low, medium and high. Low shows very low energies, down around 10-9, medium shows higher, around 10-6, and high shows higher energies around 10-3. Since the higher end of the scale shows as red, its best to start a run at low and shift to medium when most neurons are red. The scale should be shifted again when the neurons turn red at that level.
